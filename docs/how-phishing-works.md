# How Phishing Works

Phishing is a social engineering technique that attempts to trick users into taking an action, such as clicking a malicious link or entering credentials on a fake website.

## Attack elements

- **Spoofed sender**: The attacker imitates a trusted source.
- **Urgent call to action**: Messages often pressure the recipient to act quickly.
- **Malicious content**: Links or attachments are used to capture data.
- **Psychological triggers**: Fear, curiosity, or authority can drive behavior.

## Common phishing objectives

- credential theft
- financial fraud
- account takeover
- intelligence gathering

## Human attack surface

Employees are the primary risk vector in phishing scenarios. Common behavioral factors include:

- clicking unknown links
- using weak or reused passwords
- failing to verify sender identity
- not reporting suspicious messages

## Defensive controls

Effective phishing awareness programs combine:

- simulation exercises
- measurable training outcomes
- reporting channels
- ongoing reinforcement
- risk-based remediation

## Why simulation matters

Simulated phishing campaigns help organizations identify knowledge gaps before a real attack occurs. They enable security teams to assign focused remediation and improve user behavior over time.
